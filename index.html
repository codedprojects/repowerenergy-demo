Harnessing rivers for nation building

Repower Energy Development Corporation

Repower Energy Development Corporation (REDC) is a 100% owned subsidiary of Pure Energy Holdings Corporation. REDC is renewable energy developer of run of river hydropower projects through a "clustered approach method" where several projects are in a target area providing for shared economies of scale through common infrastructure, reducing the cost of development and improving the bottom line.

Advantages of run of river hydropower Projects

Storage plants which utilize dams have implementation problems related to social acceptability because in some cases, entire barangays will be submerged under water for the project. Hence, in the Philippines, run-off-the-river plants are preferred and commonly found in most existing installations because of lesser social-related problems, foremost of which is the resistance of the people.

Enhances the Forest Cover of the Watershed

The Department of Energy (DOE) requires a watershed improvement plan in the final conclusion of the operations contract. Additionally, run of river hydropower developers are intrinsically incentivized to preserve the forest cover in order to maintain both water quality and quantity.

CO2 Savings

A 1 MW Mini-hydro plant saves 2,640 Tons of CO2 per year

Car Equivalent

This saving is equivalent to the emissions of 600 cars

Oil Import Savings

The plant saves the Philippine government US$ 500,000 / year / MW in oil imports

Fossil Fuel Savings

It also saves 1.32 million liters of fossil-based fuel

Government Incentives

The government provides incentives for hydropower projects under RA 9513
